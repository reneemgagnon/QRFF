# Commercial Licensing

Commercial use of Quaternion Ruffle Field (QRFF) requires a separate written license from the author.

## What counts as commercial use (examples)
- Using QRFF (code, weights, or derived implementations) in a product or service
- Offering QRFF outputs via API, SaaS, on-device features, or paid consulting deliverables
- Internal use at a for-profit company that supports revenue, product development, or customer work

## What is generally OK without a commercial license
- Noncommercial academic research and evaluation
- Reproducibility work and benchmarking in a noncommercial context
- Publishing papers, blog posts, or demos that are not monetized

## To obtain a commercial license
Open a GitHub issue labeled "commercial-license" (or contact the maintainer privately).
info@maplebrainhealth.com

Include:
- Intended use case
- Deployment context (internal, SaaS, on-device, etc)
- Organization name
- Timeline and scale
